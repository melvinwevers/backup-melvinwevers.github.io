Melvin Wevers

I like to apply computation to answer humanities questions

For my Ph.D. research, I examined how the United States emerged as a reference culture in Dutch public discourse on consumer goods. For this I used extensive digitzed newspaper collection of the National Library of the Netherlands. By computational tools with more traditional hermeneutical methods, I explored, analyzed, and visualized cultural patterns in over 50 million newspaper articles and almost 20 million advertisements.

On September 15, 2017 at 12.45, I will defend my Ph.D. in Utrecht.

Right now, I'm working as researcher-in-residence at the National Library. In this six-month research project, I explored how non-textual features of advertisements can be used for historical analysis. This involves size and position of ads, but also the use of neural networks to identity visual trends and objects in advertisements.
